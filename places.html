Places to Visit in Nepal

Pokhara

Pokhara is a beautiful lakeside city known for its stunning natural scenery, adventure activities, and views of the Annapurna mountain range. It’s a gateway for trekkers heading to the Annapurna Circuit and Base Camp.

Landmarks to Visit:

  • Phewa Lake
  • Devi’s Fall
  • World Peace Pagoda

Hotels to Stay:

  • Hotel Pokhara Grande - $80 per day
  • Waterfront Resort - $100 per day
  • Temple Tree Resort - $90 per day

Kathmandu

Kathmandu, the capital city, is a cultural and historical hub known for its temples, monasteries, and unique architecture. It’s the perfect place to explore Nepali history and spirituality.

Landmarks to Visit:

  • Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple)
  • Pashupatinath Temple
  • Durbar Square

Hotels to Stay:

  • Hotel Yak & Yeti - $120 per day
  • Hyatt Regency - $150 per day
  • Thamel Eco Resort - $70 per day

Mustang

Mustang is a remote region with breathtaking landscapes, ancient caves, and Tibetan culture. It’s often called the “Last Forbidden Kingdom” and offers a unique travel experience.

Landmarks to Visit:

  • Lo Manthang
  • Jomsom
  • Muktinath Temple

Hotels to Stay:

  • Hotel Om’s Home - $60 per day
  • Mustang Eco Resort - $80 per day
  • Red House Lodge - $50 per day

Lumbini

Lumbini is the birthplace of Buddha and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It’s a spiritual and serene location that attracts visitors from all over the world.

Landmarks to Visit:

  • Maya Devi Temple
  • World Peace Pagoda
  • Lumbini Museum

Hotels to Stay:

  • Lumbini Buddha Garden - $70 per day
  • Siddhartha Guest House - $50 per day
  • Lumbini Hokke Hotel - $80 per day

Rara Lake

Rara Lake, the largest lake in Nepal, is surrounded by stunning mountain scenery and peaceful nature. It's an off-the-beaten-path destination ideal for nature lovers.

Landmarks to Visit:

  • Rara National Park
  • Talcha Airport
  • Mugu Village

Hotels to Stay:

  • Danfe Lodge - $30 per day
  • Rara Eco Resort - $40 per day
  • Hotel Rara View - $35 per day
