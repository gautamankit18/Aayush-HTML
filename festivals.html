Festivals of Nepal

Dashain

Dashain is the longest and most important festival in Nepal, celebrated by Hindus with great joy and family gatherings. It symbolizes the victory of good over evil and lasts for 15 days, featuring rituals and gatherings that strengthen family bonds.

Significant Rituals:

  • Ghatasthapana - Planting of barley seeds for ritual worship
  • Fulpati - Procession of sacred flowers and offerings
  • Tika - Family elders apply a mixture of rice, yogurt, and red color to bless younger members

Tihar

Tihar, also known as the Festival of Lights, is celebrated with five days of worship for animals, including crows, dogs, and cows. The festival is marked by oil lamps, colorful decorations, and worship of Laxmi, the goddess of wealth.

Significant Rituals:

  • Kukur Tihar - Honoring dogs for their loyalty
  • Laxmi Puja - Worship of the goddess of wealth, with households illuminated by oil lamps
  • Bhai Tika - Celebration of the bond between brothers and sisters

Teej

Teej is a significant festival for Nepali Hindu women, marked by fasting, prayer, and vibrant gatherings. Women pray for their husbands' long lives and for marital bliss, wearing red attire and performing traditional dances.

Significant Rituals:

  • Dar Khane Din - A gathering for feasting before the fasting day
  • Fasting - Married women fast and pray for the well-being of their spouses
  • Dance and Singing - Traditional songs and dances in groups, dressed in red sarees
