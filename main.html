Geography

Nepal is a landlocked country situated between China and India. It is home to eight of the worldâ€™s highest peaks, including Mount Everest. The geography of Nepal varies dramatically, from the tropical plains of the Terai in the south to the towering Himalayan mountains in the north.

History

Nepal has a rich and diverse history, deeply rooted in Hindu and Buddhist traditions. It was unified by King Prithvi Narayan Shah in the 18th century and became a democratic republic in 2008 after years of monarchy. The country has preserved its cultural heritage through temples, palaces, and ancient sites.

Unique Features

Nepal is known for its vibrant festivals, diverse ethnic groups, and welcoming people. The capital city, Kathmandu, is famous for its UNESCO World Heritage sites, while places like Lumbini, the birthplace of Buddha, attract spiritual seekers from around the world.
